About the artist

I’m Alex Preston, an artist based in Hertfordshire, UK, focused on the internal geometry of everyday technology. I disassemble devices like Walkmans, cameras, disk drives and CD players, then arrange every component into precise, blueprint-inspired compositions.

Each piece starts at the workbench: careful teardown, cleaning and cataloguing every screw, spring and circuit. From there, I map out a layout that balances engineering accuracy with graphic clarity, turning hidden mechanisms into wall-ready artwork.

Private commissions

Custom deconstructed tech art built around your device or idea.

STEP 01

Send the brief

Tell me which device you’d like to feature (or what type of device you are interested in), how you want to display it, and any attachment you have to it – story, brand, model, era.

STEP 02

Spec & quote

I’ll confirm what’s possible with your device, propose a layout size and style, and send a clear quote and timeframe. A deposit secures your slot.

STEP 03

Disassembly & layout

The device is fully disassembled, cleaned and organised. Components are arranged with blueprint-like precision on a backing board or prepared for high-resolution print.

STEP 04

Final artwork

You’ll receive the finished piece (framed physical work or print-ready digital files) along with a simple annotation key describing the device.
